Phase 1 · 2020–2021
Pandemic Suppression
1.1% → 2.8%
COVID-19 lockdowns suppressed demand, keeping inflation at historic lows in 2020 (1.1%). As economies reopened and supply chains strained, prices began rising through 2021, ending the year at 6.4% in December.
Phase 2 · 2022
Energy Crisis Shock
Peaked at 17.1%
Russia's invasion of Ukraine triggered a European energy crisis. Netherlands — heavily reliant on natural gas for heating and industry — was hit hard. Gas prices on TTF hub surged 10×. The crisis accelerated Groningen gas field phase-out, further tightening supply.
Phase 3 · 2023
Sharp Disinflation
17.1% → -1.0%
Energy prices collapsed due to a mild winter, LNG imports, and demand destruction. The base effect from 2022's extreme prices created a remarkable statistical deflation in Sept–Oct 2023 (-0.3% and -1.0%). Core inflation remained elevated at 4–5%.
Phase 4 · 2024–2025
Sticky Normalization
3.2% → 3.0%
Inflation stabilized in the 2.5–4% range — above the ECB's 2% target but manageable. Services inflation remained sticky (wages, rents, hospitality). Energy prices rebounded mildly in late 2024. The Netherlands outperformed the EU average slightly in 2025.
